## Part I — Sea Lice Management Thresholds

### Article 8. Treatment Obligation — Sea Lice (*Lepeophtheirus salmonis*)

**§ 8.1 — Mandatory treatment threshold**

An operator shall initiate a documented, veterinary-approved treatment program within 7 calendar days when the mean count of adult female sea lice (*Lepeophtheirus salmonis*) exceeds **0.5 adult female lice per fish** at any sampling point within the production site.

This threshold applies to all Atlantic salmon (*Salmo salar*) and rainbow trout (*Oncorhynchus mykiss*) held in open-net marine installations.

**§ 8.2 — Sampling protocol**

Sampling shall be carried out at minimum every 7 days from 1 January to 31 December. A minimum of 20 fish per cage must be examined. The result is expressed as the arithmetic mean of adult female lice per fish across all sampled cages on the site.

**§ 8.3 — Regulatory escalation**

If lice counts exceed **1.0 adult female lice per fish**, the operator must:
1. Notify the Norwegian Food Safety Authority (Mattilsynet) within 24 hours.
2. Halt introduction of new fish to the site.
3. Submit a written treatment plan within 48 hours of the threshold being confirmed.

**§ 8.4 — Prohibition on harvest deferral**

An operator may not defer mandatory treatment solely on the basis of fish market conditions or anticipated price improvements. Biological welfare obligations are absolute and supersede commercial considerations.

## Part II — Dissolved Oxygen Requirements

### Article 12. Minimum Dissolved Oxygen Standards

**§ 12.1 — Absolute minimum level**

The dissolved oxygen concentration at cage level must never fall below **6.0 mg/L** (approximately 70% saturation at 12°C in seawater at 33 ppt salinity). Readings below this threshold constitute an acute welfare event and require immediate corrective action.

**§ 12.2 — Operational minimum**

Operators must maintain dissolved oxygen levels above **7.0 mg/L** under normal production conditions. Sustained readings between 7.0 and 6.0 mg/L trigger a mandatory 4-hour monitoring escalation, where readings must be taken every 30 minutes and logged in the site welfare journal.

**§ 12.3 — Warning threshold for automated systems**

For sites equipped with continuous monitoring systems (which are mandatory for farms exceeding 780 tonnes maximum allowable biomass), an automated alert must be generated when dissolved oxygen falls below **9.0 mg/L**. This alert must be transmitted to the designated duty officer within 5 minutes of the threshold being crossed.

**§ 12.4 — Corrective measures**

When dissolved oxygen falls below the operational minimum (§ 12.2):
- Feeding must be suspended immediately.
- Supplemental aeration or oxygenation equipment must be activated.
- If oxygen levels cannot be restored above 7.0 mg/L within 2 hours, emergency harvesting protocols must be assessed.

## Part III — Water Temperature Constraints

### Article 14. Temperature Safety Limits

**§ 14.1 — Upper temperature threshold**

Salmon farming operations must suspend routine feeding when water temperature exceeds **18°C**. Operations at sustained temperatures above 18°C increase susceptibility to gill disease, amoebic gill disease (AGD), and create conditions favorable for sea lice population explosions.

**§ 14.2 — Lice risk escalation at elevated temperatures**

At water temperatures above **14°C**, the sea lice development cycle accelerates significantly (generation time reduces from ~8 weeks at 10°C to ~4 weeks at 14°C). Operators must increase lice sampling frequency to every 4 days when water temperature exceeds 14°C.

**§ 14.3 — Harvest obligation at temperature extremes**

If water temperature remains above 18°C for more than 5 consecutive days and lice counts are approaching 0.4 adult female lice per fish (i.e., within 80% of the treatment threshold), the operator must conduct an emergency biological risk assessment. The results must be submitted to Mattilsynet within 48 hours.

## Part IV — Mortality Reporting

### Article 16. Daily Mortality Registration

**§ 16.1 — Mandatory daily count**

All dead fish must be removed from cages daily. The mortality count must be recorded in the electronic operating journal (Akvakulturregisteret) before 08:00 the following morning.

**§ 16.2 — Elevated mortality alert threshold**

When cumulative mortality in any single cage exceeds **0.5% of estimated fish population over a 48-hour period**, or when single-day absolute mortality exceeds **50 individual fish**, the operator must:
1. Conduct an immediate post-mortem examination of a minimum of 5 individuals.
2. Contact the site's designated veterinarian within 4 hours.
3. Document findings in the welfare journal.

**§ 16.3 — Notifiable mortality events**

If post-mortem examination reveals evidence of ISA (Infectious Salmon Anaemia), IHN (Infectious Haematopoietic Necrosis), or any other OIE-listed notifiable disease, the operator must immediately notify Mattilsynet. All fish movements on and off the site are suspended pending official investigation.

## Enforcement Notes

Violations of Articles 8, 12, and 16 may result in:
- Suspension of the aquaculture licence for the affected production site.
- Fines calculated per day of violation (typically NOK 50,000–500,000 per day).
- Criminal liability under the Norwegian Animal Welfare Act (§ 37) for sustained negligence resulting in mass mortality.

Compliance records must be retained for a minimum of **5 years** and made available to Fiskeridirektoratet inspectors on demand.
